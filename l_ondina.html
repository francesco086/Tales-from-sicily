L'ondina

The little wave

La notte era tiepida e tranquilla, ma senza luna; nel cupissimo cielo scintillavano le stelle, con un tremolio che pareva si volessero spegnere al minimo soffio. La dolce brezza che saliva dal mare, e faceva dondolare lievemente le cime degli alberi sulla spiaggia, non giungeva alle stelle. Ed esse spandevano intorno una luce tenue, dentro la quale si confondevano i contorni delle cose come se il mistero le avesse generate.

The night was warm and peaceful, but moonless; the stars were glittering in the dark sky, flickering as if the slightest breath would suffice to turn them off. A sweet breeze was coming from the sea and it was gently swinging the treetops at the beach, without being able to reach the stars. The stars' light was feeble and caused hazy outlines, as if every object was coming from the mystery itself.

Die Nacht war mild und ruhig, aber mondlos, die Sterne leuchteten am pechschwarzen Himmel, flackernd, als wenn der kleinste Hauch sie auslöschen könnte. Doch die sanfte Brise, die vom Meer her wehte und die Baumwipfel am Strand leicht bewegte, erreichte die Sterne nicht. Diese tauchten die Umgebung in ein mattes Licht, in dem alle Konturen verschwammen, als ob ein Mysterium die Dinge geschaffen hätte.

Non era ancora notte profonda, ma la città pareva deserta; le scolte forse sonnecchiavano nelle torri; e le mura della Kalsa pareva dormissero anche loro.

It was not the dead of night yet, but the city seemed desert; the sentries in the towers were probably sleeping; and the Kalsa's walls appeared as if they were sleeping too.

Es war noch nicht tiefste Nacht, doch die Stadt schien wie ausgestorben; die Wachen im Turm schliefen wahrscheinlich und die Mauern, die das arabische Viertel Kalsa umgaben, schienen ebenfalls zu schlummern.

Il porto della Cala era chiuso da pesanti catene di ferro, e le galee riposavano, con un lieve dondolio delle antenne, sul molle letto, al sicuro. Forse in quell'ora il magnifico e potente Ruggero, re di Sicilia, studiava gli astri coi suoi astrologi arabi e compilava con Edrisi la sua geografia; egli forse vegliava, ma i cittadini di Palermo dormivano: gli arabi nella Kalsa dalle quattro porte, gli ebrei nella Juzeta, gli amalfitani presso la porta dei Patitelli, i greci intorno alla loro parrocchia di San Nicolò, sullo sprone che cingeva il porto; i signori normanni e italiani nella via Marmorea, la plebe nell'Albergheria e in Seralcadi, i soldati nelle Moschee, alla Galca. Tutti dormivano. Chi non aveva sonno era messer Gilberto, milite normanno, al quale era stata affidata la custodia della torre di Baich e della Porta di mare.

The Cala's harbour was locked by heavy iron chains, and the galleys were resting safely, while the yards were rocking lightly. Meanwhile the magnificent and powerful Ruggero, king of Sicily, was perhaps reading the stars with his arab astrologers and filling in some geographical map together with Edrisi; he was possibly awake, but the dwellers of Palermo were asleep: the arabs in the Kalsa with its four gates, the jews in the Juzeta, the amalfitans at the Patitelli's gate, the greeks in the surroundings of their San Nicolò parish, on the spur that enclosed the harbour; the italian and norman lords were asleep in the Marmorea's Street, the mob at the Albergheria and in Seralcadi, the soldiers at the Galca. Everyone was sleeping. The only one who couldn't sleep was sir Gilberto, a norman soldier, who was entrusted with the task of taking care of the Baich tower and of the sea's gate.

Der Hafen war mit schweren Eisenketten abgesperrt und die Galeeren, in Sicherheit, ruhten mit sanft schaukelnden Rahen in ihren weichen Betten. Zu dieser Zeit beobachtete der großartige und mächtige Ruggero, König von Sizilien, vielleicht mit seinen arabischen Astrologen die Sterne und befüllte zusammen mit dem Gelehrten Edrisi geographische Karten – er war möglicherweise wach, aber alle anderen Bewohner Palermos schliefen: die Araber in Kalsa mit seinen vier Toren, die Juden in der Juzeta, die Amalfiter bei der Porta dei Patitelli, die Griechen rund um ihre Pfarrei San Nicolò, deren Ausläufer an den Hafen grenzte, die italienischen und normannischen Adligen in der Via Marmorea, der Pöbel in Albergheria und Seralcadi, die Soldaten in der Galca. Alle schliefen. Der einzige, der nicht schlafen konnte, war Gilberto, ein normannischer Soldat, der dafür verantwortlich war, den Baich-Turm und das Tor zum Meer zu bewachen.

Avea posto le guardie dietro la saracinesca; si era assicurato che ogni cosa era in ordine, e se ne era andato a letto. Ma le guardie che avevano a vegliare, dormivano, ed egli che voleva dormire, vegliava suo malgrado. Sentiva caldo. Aveva un bel voltarsi nel letto, i cuscini gli parevano di brace; la volta bassa della stanza l'opprimeva; l'aria chiusa lo soffocava. Allora si alzò e aperse la finestra; si affacciò e guardò il mare ampio, solenne, fresco. Le spume argentine e il mormorio soave dei flutti lo chiamavano: egli lo sentiva; gli pareva che dal fondo di quelle acque una voce lo invitasse: "Vieni, o figlio di Normandia, vieni; qui è la frescura, qui è la dolcezza; vieni, ti comporrò un letto di alghe e ti cullerò nel sonno; vieni!".

He placed the guards behind the portcullis; he made himself sure that everything was in order, and he went to bed. But the guards, who had to watch over, were asleep and he, who just wanted to sleep, was awake. He was feeling warm. Tossing and turning in the bed was useless: the pillows seemed made of embers to him; the vaulted ceiling was too low, oppressing him; the foul air was stifling him. Eventually he stood up and opened the window; he leaned out and looked at the broad, solemn, fresh sea. The silvered foams and the sweet murmuring of the waves were calling him: he could hear that; it seemed to him as a voice from the sea bed was inviting him: "Come, oh son of Normandie, come; here is the coolness, here is the sweetness; come, I will compose a bed of seaweed for you and I will lulled you to sleep; come!".

Er wies die Wachen an, sich hinter dem heruntergelassenen Gatter aufzustellen, er überprüfte, ob alles seine Ordnung hatte, dann ging er zu Bett. Doch die Wachen, die Wache halten sollten, schliefen, während er, der schlafen wollte, unfreiwillig wach lag. Ihm war heiß. Er wälzte sich von einer Seite auf die andere, aber vergeblich: Die Kissen erschienen ihm wie glühende Kohlen, die niedrige Gewölbedecke erdrückte ihn, die schwüle Luft gab ihm das Gefühl zu ersticken. Schließlich stand er auf, öffnete ein Fenster, lehnte sich nach draußen und schaute über das weite, majestätische, kühle Meer. Die silbrige Gischt und das liebliche Rauschen der Wellen riefen ihn: Er konnte es genau hören, es war, als ob eine Stimme vom Meeresgrund ihn einlud: "Komm, Sohn der Normandie, komm; hier ist es kühl, hier ist es süß, komm, ich werde dir ein Bett aus Algen bereiten und dich in den Schlaf wiegen, komm!""

Messer Gilberto guardò per un pezzo quel mare sereno, carezzevole, pieno di misteriosa promessa, e sentì più che mai il peso della volta che l'opprimeva e l'afa insopportabile della stanza: e allora, borbottando, indossò la tunicella e uscì. "Sia che ci voglia, vo' a prendere un bagno.".

Sir Gilberto looked for a while at that sea, calm, gentle, full of mysterious promise, and he could not live with the weight of the oppressive ceiling and the sultriness of his room anymore: and then, while mumbling to himself, he put his tunic on, and went out. "I definitely need this, I am going for a swim".

Gilberto schaute eine Weile auf das ruhige, einladende Meer voller mysteriöser Versprechungen und auf einmal hielt er die erdrückende Enge der Gewölbedecke und die unerträgliche Schwüle seines Zimmers nicht mehr aus: Daraufhin zog er seine Tunika über, ging nach draußen und murmelte: "Ich muss jetzt eine Runde Schwimmen gehen".

Diede uno strattone alla guardia che sonnecchiava alla porta. "Che ti venga il malanno, poltrone! Su! Destati!".

He jerked the guard at the gate who was dozing. "Darn you, lazybones! Wake up!".

Er gab dem dösenden Wachmann am Tor einen Stoß. „Verfluchter Faulpelz! Hopp! Wach auf!

Il soldato balzò in piedi, si stropicciò gli occhi, sbadigliò. "Scusate... non vi avevo veduto...". "Tira su la saracinesca, ed apri...". "Aprire? Uscite, messere?". "Fa' quello che ti dico, anima di un saraceno!".

The soldier jumped on his feet, rubbed his eyes, and yawned. "Excuse me... I did not notice you...". "Lift the shutter, and open...". "Open? Are you going out, sir?". "Do what I am telling you to do, son of a saracen!".

Der Soldat sprang auf, rieb sich die Augen und gähnte. "Tut mir Leid… ich habe Euch nicht bemerkt…" "Zieh das Gatter hoch und öffne –" "Öffnen? Geht Ihr nach draußen, Herr?" "Tu, was ich dir sage, Sohn eines Sarazenen!"

Brontolando, il soldato afferrò la manovella, sollevò la saracinesca, e aprì lo sportello. "Stai attento, che fra un'ora tornerò.". "Buona fortuna, messere" sogghignò il soldato, il quale suppose che il milite messer Gilberto andasse a un ritrovo amoroso.

Grumbling, the soldier grabbed the crank, lifted the shutter, and opened the gate. "Watch out, I will be back in one hour.". "Good luck, sir" sneered the soldier, who assumed that sir Gilberto was going out for a love affair.

Grummelnd betätigte der Soldat die Kurbel, um das Gatter zu heben, und öffnete das Tor. „Bleib wachsam, in einer Stunde bin ich zurück“. „Viel Glück, Herr“ grinste der Soldat, der annahm, dass der Hauptmann zu einem Rendezvous aufbrach.

Messer Gilberto discese alla riva, si calò dagli scogli sull'arena molle e fine, si spogliò e si tuffò nel mare con un senso di voluttà grandissima, e con meraviglia del soldato che lo aveva seguito con lo sguardo.

Sir Gilberto went down to the seashore, shined down the reef until the soft fine-grained sand, undressed himself, and dived into the sea, his soul filled with pleasure. The soldier, who was following sir Gilberto with his eyes, was surprised to see this.

Gilberto ging zur Küste hinunter, lies sich die Klippen hinab auf den weichen, feinen Sand, entkleidete sich und tauchte zur Überraschung des Soldaten, der ihm mit dem Blick gefolgt war, voll Freude in das wohltuende Meer.

Cominciò a nuotare silenziosamente, lentamente, ora adagiandosi sopra un piano, ora lasciandosi trasportare dalle onde. Ogni tanto si sommergeva, spariva, per ricomparire alcune bracciate più in là, tutto molle, coi capelli incollati, qualche filo d'erba marina fra le ciocche. Scuoteva allora il capo, e con una vigorosa spinta del piede fendeva le acque. Di quando in quando si fermava, sorreggendosi col remigar lento delle braccia, e guardava innanzi a sè le mura della città, e dietro le mura la corona dei monti, come una gran massa nera, incerta; sul capo l'immenso cielo punteggiato di stelle: un padiglione che non finiva mai; dietro di sè il mare immenso anche esso, che si confondeva col cielo in un abbraccio; e in quel mistero oscuro di cielo e di mare, egli si sentiva piccino, smarrito, soggiogato da un religioso terrore, che lo costringeva a chiudere gli occhi o a rivolgersi altrove.

He started swimming silently, slowly, sometimes laying flat, sometimes letting the waves carrying him. From time to time he was plunging, disappearing, and appearing few strokes ahead, soft, with his hairs stuck on his forehead, and few blades of seaweed in his hairs. He was then shaking his head, and slicing through the water with an energetic push of his foot. From time to time he was stopping, supporting himself out of the water using the slow movements of his arms, and he was looking at the walls of the city in front of him. Behind the walls there was a crown of mountains, that looked like a big black unclear mass; the sky above his head, all stippled with stars, seemed huge like a never-ending pavilion; behind his back, the sea seemed to have no limits, held in an embrace by the sky; plunged in that dark mystery made of the sky and the sea, he felt small, lost, subjugated by a sort of religious terror, which forced him to close his eyes and to look somewhere else.

Lautlos und langsam begann er zu schwimmen. Mal lag er flach im Wasser, mal ließ er sich von den Wellen treiben. Von Zeit zu Zeit tauchte er unter, verschwand, und tauchte ein Stück weiter vorne wieder auf, komplett durchnässt, mit Seegras zwischen den feuchten Haarsträhnen. Daraufhin schüttelte er den Kopf und pflügte mit einem kraftvollen Beinschlag durch die Wellen. Ab und an hielt er inne, hielt sich mit langsamen Armbewegungen über Wasser und betrachtete die Stadtmauer und die Bergkette, die sich wie ein schwarzes, verschwommenes Massiv dahinter erhob. Über ihm spannte sich der weite Sternenhimmel wie ein unendlicher Pavillon auf, hinter ihm das weite Meer, in einer Umarmung mit dem Himmel. Inmitten dieses dunklen Mysteriums des Himmels und des Meeres fühlte er sich klein, verloren und er wurde von einer Art religiöser Furcht befallen, die ihn zwang, seine Augen zu schließen oder sich abzuwenden.

Ma, a un tratto, trasalì. Mentre così assorto si lasciava trasportare dalle onde, qualche cosa di morbido e fine sfiorò le sue mani.

But all of a sudden, he winced. While he was lost in his thoughts and was letting the waves carrying him, something soft and thin brushed against his hands.

Plötzlich schrak er zusammen. Während er sich tief in Gedanken von den Wellen treiben ließ, streifte etwas weiches, feines seine Hände.

Non era un pesce. Strinse il pugno e lo sollevò: erano capelli; dei lunghi capelli biondi che gli si avvolgevano nella mano e nel braccio. Un brivido gelido di spavento gli corse per la persona, gli arrestò il sangue; il suo pugno si dischiuse per un attimo, ma, subito, le sue mani si unirono, afferrarono la bionda chioma emersa dalle acque, un volto e poi le spalle e il seno di una donna, che pareva morta.

It was no fish. He closed his hand and he lifted it up; long blond hairs were all winded around his hand and arm. A shiver ran down his spine, and his blood ran cold; he opened his hands only for a moment, and immediately joined them and lifted up the head of blonde hair, a face, and then the shoulders and the breast of a woman, seemingly dead.

Es war kein Fisch. Er schloss die Hand und hob sie aus dem Wasser – langes blondes Haar wickelte sich um seine Hand und seinen Arm. Ein Schauer lief ihm den Rücken hinunter und das Blut gefror in seinen Adern. Für einen kurzen Moment öffnete er die Faust, dann führte er die Hände zusammen, fasste in die blonde Mähne, die sich im Meer verteilte, und ertastete ein Gesicht und dann die Schultern und die Brust einer Frau, offenbar tot.

Vincendo il senso del terrore e il ribrezzo della morte, messer Gilberto strinse con un braccio i fianchi inerti di quella morta, e con l'altro nuotò alla riva. Quando posò i piedi, sollevò quel corpo molle e abbandonato, lo trasse dall'acqua, lo adagiò sulla sabbia. Guardò. Una commozione profonda gli rimescolò il sangue, e un'onda di pietà gli strappò un gemito di rimpianto. Non aveva mai veduto una donna così meravigliosamente bella. E giaceva come morta, col bel capo biondo reclinato sopra una spalla, tutte le membre perfette, nude, nella incosciente inverecondia della morte. Egli ebbe pietà e amore per quella beltà perduta, e la coperse con la sua tunicella; le si chinò accanto, contemplandola, non sapendo che fare. Certo ella era venuta a chiedergli sepoltura cristiana, per non essere divorata dai pesci. Ma quando era annegata? E chi l'aveva buttata in mare?

Overcoming his sense of terror and disgust of the death, sir Gilberto held the hips of the dead with one arm, and used the other arm for swimming until the shore. When he could walk again, he lifted up that lifeless body, dragged it ashore, and leaned it on the sand. He had a better look at it. A deep emotion made his blood run cold, and a rush of merci made him groan for regret. He had never seen such a beautiful lady before. And she was laying dead, with her beautiful blonde head bowed against her shoulder, with her perfect body naked, with the unconscious impudence of the dead people. He had at the same time a feeling of pity and love for the lost beauty, and he covered her with his tunic; he laid next to her, contemplating her, without knowing what to do. Surely she came to him asking for christian burial, and because she didn't want the fishes to eat her body. But when did she drown? And who threw her in the sea?

Gilberto überwand seinen Schrecken und seine Abscheu vor dem Tod, umfasste mit einem Arm die Hüften der Toten und nutzte den anderen Arm, um zum Ufer zu schwimmen. Sobald er Stehen konnte, hob er den nassen, leblosen Körper hoch, zog ihn aus dem Wasser und legte ihn auf den Sand. Er sah ihn an. Ein tiefes Gefühl durchfloss ihn und eine Welle des Mitleids ließ ihn einen Seufzer des Bedauerns ausstoßen. Nie zuvor hatte er eine so wunderschöne Frau gesehen. Und da lag sie, tot, ihr blonder Kopf gegen die Schulter gesunken, ihr perfekter Körper nackt, mit der unbewussten Schamlosigkeit des Todes. Er fühlte gleichzeitig Mitleid und Liebe für diese verlorene Schönheit. Er bedeckte sie mit seiner Tunika, legte sich neben sie und bewunderte sie, ohne zu wissen, was er tun sollte. Sie war sicherlich zu ihm gekommen, um ein christliches Begräbnis zu erhalten, anstatt von den Fischen zerfressen zu werden. Aber wann war sie ertrunken? Und wer hatte sie ins Meer geworfen?

Ma, a un tratto, messer Gilberto gettò un grido di spavento; gli parve di vedere scorrere un brivido a fior di pelle sulle membra della morta: si chinò su di lei. In quell'istante la luna sorgeva sulle acque, diffondendo intorno una luce blanda; egli sorprese un lieve movimento delle nari, gli parve di udire un fievole soffio. "Gran Dio! È viva!".

Suddenly sir Gilberto screamed with terror; it seemed to him that a shiver was running through that corpse: he then bent over her. In that moment the moon was rising on the water, diffusing a soft light around; he caught a slight movement of the nostrils, and he believed to hear a soft breath. "Great Lord! She is alive!".

Plötzlich schrie Gilberto erschrocken auf: ihm schien, als sehe er einen leichten Schauer durch die Glieder der Toten fahren und er beugte sich über sie. In diesem Moment erschien der Mond über dem Wasser und tauchte die Umgebung in ein sanftes Licht. Er nahm eine leichte Bewegung der Nasenflügel wahr und glaubte, ein schwaches Atmen zu vernehmen. „Großer Gott! Sie lebt!“

Cominciò a stropicciarla forte, la sollevò per le braccia, volto contro volto, come se avesse voluto affrettare il ritorno della vita. Avrebbe voluto chiamare e non poteva, perché la commozione gli fermava la voce nella gola: guardò la luna come per invocarne l'aiuto, guardò il cielo con una preghiera intensa sugli occhi...

He started to scrape her energetically, and lifted her up from the arms, his face against her face, as if in this way he could speed up her return to life. He wanted to call for help but he couldn't, because the emotion was blocking his voice; he looked at the moon as if he could invoke its help, and looked at the sky with an intense prayer was written all over his face...

Er begann, sie kräftig zu reiben, er hob sie an den Armen hoch, Gesicht an Gesicht, als könne er so das Leben schneller in sie zurückholen. Er wollte schreien, doch die Emotionen schnürten ihm die Kehle zu. Er blickte zum Mond, als ob er ihn um Hilfe bitten wollte, er blickte mit flehenden Augen zum Himmel...

In quel momento la bellissima sospirò, poi mosse un braccio, poi aperse gli occhi; mentre messer Gilberto con le mani giunte, muto ora per la gioia, seguiva ansiosamente il ritorno della vita in quelle membra stupende. In ginocchio ancora, mentre ella seduta cercava pudicamente di coprirsi meglio, messer Gilberto gli domandò: "Come vi chiamate? Chi siete?".

In that moment the beauty sighed, then moved an arm, and finally opened her eyes; in the meantime sir Gilberto, with his hands joined, was speechless for the joy, and was following anxiously that wonderful body coming back to life. While she was still on her knees, trying to maidenly cover herself the best she could, sir Gilberto asked her: "What's your name? Who are you?".

Da stieß die Schöne einen Seufzer aus, bewegte einen Arm und öffnete schließlich die Augen, während Gilberto, mit verschränkten Händen und sprachlos vor Freude, besorgt verfolgte, wie das Leben in diesen wundervollen Körper zurückkehrte. Noch immer kniend versuchte sie verschämt, ihren Körper so gut es ging zu bedecken, während Gilberto sie fragte: „Wie heißt Ihr? Wer seid Ihr?“

Ma ella lo guardò dolcemente, e non rispose. "Come siete caduta in mare?".

But she looked at him gently, and didn't answer. "How did you fall in the sea?".

Aber sie sah ihn nur freundlich an und antwortete nicht. „Wie seid Ihr ins Meer gefallen?“

Ella lo guardava dolcemente e taceva. "Perché non rispondete? Siete cristiana? Siete saracena? Comprendete la mia lingua? Rispondetemi, per amor di Dio!".

She was looking at him kindly and silently. "Why don't you answer me? Are you christian? Are you saracenic? Do you understand my language? For God's sake, answer me!".

Sie sah ihn weiter freundlich und stumm an. „Warum antwortet Ihr nicht? Seid Ihr Christin? Seid Ihr Sarazenin? Versteht Ihr meine Sprache? So antwortet doch, in Gottes Namen!“

Una nube oscurò per un attimo i grandi occhi azzurri della bellissima, ma ella non rispose; lo guardava sempre, con un malinconico sorriso, con una tenerezza profonda. Allora egli impallidì, si sentì commuovere, si sentì mancare, e gridò: "Ma siete dunque muta?".

For a short moment a cloud darkened the big blue eyes of the beauty, but she did non answer; she kept looking at him, with a melancholy smile, with a deep tenderness. He then turned pale, he felt touched, his strength failed him, and he shouted: "Are you dumb perhaps?".

Einen Moment lang verdunkelte eine Wolke die großen blauen Augen der Schönheit, aber sie antwortete nicht, sie blickte ihn weiter mit einem melancholischen Lächeln voll tiefer Zärtlichkeit an. Da erbleichte er. Er fühlte sich gerührt, er fühlte sich verloren, und er rief: „Seid Ihr wohl stumm?“

Ed ella non rispose neppure, ma continuò a guardarlo dolcemente e malinconicamente. E allor messer Gilberto, dopo un minuto di silenzio, la costrinse ad alzarsi, e le disse con tristezza: "Venite; voi non potete restare qui.". E si mossero.

Again, she didn't answer, but she kept looking at him sweetly and melancholy instead. After a minute of silence sir Gilberto compelled her to stand up, and told her with sadness: "Come with me; you can't stay here.". And they moved.

Sie antwortete immer noch nicht, sondern sah ihn weiterhin freundlich und melancholisch an. Nach einer Minute des Schweigens zwang er sie, aufzustehen und meinte traurig: „Kommt, hier könnt Ihr nicht bleiben“. Und sie gingen los.

Quando il soldato di guardia vide ritornare messer Gilberto con una donna nuda, stupì: "Oh, che dà codesti frutti il mare?". "Lascia stare", rispose malinconicamente messer Gilberto, "ne sono ancora rimescolato; l'ho tratta che pareva morta...". "Ma come?". "Chi ne sa nulla? S'è annegata? L'hanno buttata per assassinarla? Non ho potuto saper nulla... Credo sia muta..."

When the soldier on guard saw sir Gilberto with a nude woman, wondered: "Oh, does the sea bear such kind of fruit?". "Don't mention it", answered gloomily sir Gilberto, "I still have my blood cold; When I found her she seemed dead...". "How is it possible?". "Who knows? She drowned? Someone threw her in the sea for murdering her? I couldn't get any information... I believe she is dumb..."

Als der Wachsoldat Gilberto mit einer nackten Frau zurückkommen sah, staunte er: „Oh, trägt das Meer solche Früchte?“. „Sei still“, antwortete Gilbert düster, „ich bin noch ganz neben mir, als ich sie fand, schien sie tot…“. „Wie das?“. „Wer weiß? Sie ist ertrunken? Man hat sie ertränkt, um sie zu ermorden? Ich habe nichts herausgefunden… Ich glaube, sie ist stumm…“

Era entrato, s'era fatto dare uno di quei grandi mantelli saraceni che si trovavano nella torre e ne aveva coperto la donna. Ella tremava, stringendosi nel manto, scuotendo i capelli, che parevano tramutati in ruscelli.

He went in, got one of those large saracenic mantels that could be found in the tower, and he used it for covering the lady. She was trembling, wrapping herself in the mantle and shaking her hairs, which now looked similar to streamlets.

Er ging hinein, holte einen der langen Sarazenermäntel, die im Turm zu finden waren, und bedeckte damit den Körper der Frau. Sie zitterte, wickelte sich in den Mantel, und schüttelte ihr Haar, das sie wie ein Bach umfloss.

Bisognava trovare un alloggio per quella notte. Ma dove? Dov'erano i suoi parenti? E chi era?

It was necessary to find her an accommodation for that night. But where? Where were her parents? And who was her?

Er musste eine Unterkunft für diese Nacht finden. Aber wo? Wo waren ihre Eltern? Und wer war sie überhaupt?

Certo non poteva andare attorno per la città con una donna giovane, bella e nuda; né poteva, d'altronde, ricoverarla nella torre, fra i soldati, senza esporla a scherzi grossolani e brutali.

Surely he could not go around the city with a young, beautiful, and nude girl; nor he could, however, shelter her in the tower without exposing her to the crude and beastly jokes of the soldiers.

Keinesfalls konnte er mit einem jungen, schönen und nackten Mädchen durch die Stadt laufen, doch genauso wenig konnte er sie im Turm mit den Soldaten unterbringen, wo sie deren groben und brutalen Scherzen ausgesetzt wäre.

Non c'era che ricoverarla a casa. Messer Gilberto abitava a due passi, vicino la Fonte della Salute, sulle mura del Cassaro che dominavano il punto in cui lo stagno del Papireto s'immetteva nel porto. Egli era scapolo, nessuno avrebbe molestato la poverina.

There was no other solution but to shelter her at home. Sir Gilberto house was within a short walk, near the Fonte della Salute, on the Cassaro's walls, which were dominating the point where the Papireto's pond flowed into the harbour. He was a bachelor, therefore nobody would have harassed the poor little thing.

Die einzige Lösung war, sie in seinem Haus unterzubringen. Gilberto wohnte nur einen Steinwurf entfernt, nahe dem Fonte della Salute, an der Cassaro-Mauer, die den Punkt beherrschte, an dem der Papireto-Teich in den Hafen mündete. Er war Junggeselle, daher würde niemand die Arme belästigen.

"Ora, ora ritornerò", disse alla guardia. E, stesa la mano alla donna, aggiunse: "Venite, madonna.".

"I will go back home", he told to the guard. Then he reached out his hand toward the lady and added: "Come with me, madonna.".

„Ich gehe nach Hause“, sagte er zum Wachmann. Die Hand zu dem Mädchen ausstreckend ergänzte er: „Kommt, meine Dame“.

Ella sospirò e gli tenne dietro. Il suo passo era lieve, pareva che sorvolasse sulla terra; non si sentiva che il fruscio del manto. Entrarono nella via dei Patitelli. Messer Gilberto guardava la giovane che gli veniva a fianco, e la sua commozione e la sua pietà si andavano mutando in un altro sentimento. La luna era alta, ed egli vedeva tutta la bella persona avvolta in una luce mite e uguale. Le tempie gli martellavano, il sangue gli saliva a ondate sul volto; un sapore indefinito gli empiva la bocca; a mano a mano che s'avvicinava a casa sua la febbre cresceva, tutta la sua carne ne vibrava.

She sighed and walked after him. Her steps were delicate, it seemed that she was overflying the ground; nothing could be heard but the swoosh of the mantle. They entered in Patitelli's Street. Sir Gilberto was looking at the girl who was walking alongside him, and his concern and mercy were changing into a different feeling. The moon was high in the sky, and he could see the whole figure enveloped by a soft and uniform light. His temples were throbbing, the blood was flushing in his face in waves; an undefined taste was filling his mouth; the closer to home he was getting, the more his fever was growing, and all his flesh was quivering.

Sie seufzte und folgte ihm. Ihre Schritte waren leicht, es schien als schwebte sie über dem Boden, nichts als das Rascheln des Mantels war zu hören. Sie betraten die Via dei Patitelli. Gilberto beobachtete das Mädchen, das neben ihm ging, und seine Rührung und sein Mitleid verwandelten sich nach und nach in andere Gefühle. Der Mond stand hoch am Himmel und tauchte ihren schönen Körper in ein sanftes, gleichmäßiges Licht. Gilbertos Schläfen pochten, sein Blut floss stoßweise durch sein Gesicht und ein undefinierbarer Geschmack breitete sich in seinem Mund aus. Je näher sie seinem Haus kamen, desto intensiver wurde sein Fieber, sein ganzer Körper bebte.

Quando giunse, la mano tremava nel cercare la toppa; tremava nell'accendere la lampada; e tremava la sua voce nel dirle: "Entrate!"; tremava nell'indicarle il letto, dicendo: "Potete riposarvi... nessuno verrà a turbare il vostro sonno.".

When he arrived, his hand was trembling while searching for the keyhole; was trembling while turning on the lamp; also his voice was trembling while he was telling her: "Come in!"; was trembling while saying: "You can rest... nobody will come to disturb your sleep.".

Als sie ankamen zitterten seine Hände, während er das Schlüsselloch suchte und die Lampe anmachte, und zitterte seine Stimme, während er sagte: „Tretet ein!“, und während er ihr das Bett zeigte und sagte: „Ihr könnt hier ausruhen… Niemand wird euren Schlaf stören.“

Le ultime parole gli si spensero nella bocca come soffocate. Ella non rispose; restò in mezzo alla stanza, con le braccia abbandonate lungo la persona, immobile come una statua, ma il forte ansare del seno spingeva con moto ritmico le pieghe del manto.

His last words faded away in his own throat, as muffled. She didn't answer; she stayed in the middle of the room, with her arms laying along her figure, still as a statue; however, her breast was pressing against the mantle's folds rhythmically because of her heavy gasping.

Die letzten Worte erstickten in seiner Kehle. Sie antwortete nicht. Sie blieb in der Mitte des Raumes stehen, die Arme um sich geschlungen, starr wie eine Statue, doch ihre Brust hob und senkte die Falten des Mantels im Rhythmus ihrer starken Atmung.

"Volete che io me ne vada?", le domandò messer Gilberto, con la voce sopraffatta da un dolore acuto.

"Do you want me to leave?", asked sir Gilberto, while an acute sorrow was getting better of his voice.

„Wollt Ihr, dass ich gehe?”, fragte Gilberto, die Stimme von einem plötzlichen Schmerz bedrückt.

Ella lo guardò coi limpidi occhi azzurri, lungamente, intensamente; ed egli sentì quello sguardo penetrargli dentro le carni come una spada; sentì meno venir la vita, le cadde ai ginocchi tenendole le mani, non potendo staccare gli occhi suoi dagli occhi di lei, subendo una fascinazione nuova, sentendosi come preso in un incantamento. Sentì le belle mani posarsi lievemente sulle sue, sentì il calore di un respiro nel suo volto; sentì le pupille azzurre così vicine, che gli pareva averle dentro le sue. Non vide più nulla. Una nube lo avvolse, avvolse la bella donna, avvolse la camera... E nel silenzio si diffuse un gemito.

She gazed at him with her blue liquid eyes for a long time, deeply; and he perceived that gaze penetrating into his flesh as a sword; he felt his life abandoning him, and he dropped on his knees while holding her hands, without being able to avert his eyes from hers, subjected to a new kind of bewitching, like if he was under a spell. He felt her beautiful hands laying on his, he felt the warmth of a breath over his face; he felt her blue eyes so close that he thought they were inside his own eyes. He could not see anything anymore. A cloud covered him, covered the beautiful lady, covered the room... And a moan rose from the silence.

Sie blickte ihn mit ihren klaren, blauen Augen an, lang und intensiv, und er fühlte sich, als ob ihn dieser Blick wie ein Schwert durchbohrte; er fühlte, wie das Leben aus ihm wich, er ging in die Knie und fasste ihre Hände, unfähig, seine Augen von ihren zu lösen, einer neuartigen Faszination unterworfen, wie von einem Zauber erfasst. Er spürte ihre schönen Hände auf seinen ruhen, er spürte die Wärme ihres Atems auf seinem Gesicht. Ihre blauen Augen fühlten sich so nah an, dass es ihm vorkam, als lägen sie in seinen eigenen. Er sah nichts anderes mehr. Eine Wolke umhüllte ihn, umhüllte die schöne Frau, umhüllte den ganzen Raum… Und aus der Stille ertönte ein Stöhnen.

Per tutto il giorno la casa di messer Gilberto restò chiusa; nessuno lo vide; nessuno seppe quale mistero nascondesse. Le belle e candide braccia della fascinatrice lo tennero stretto in un sogno. Ma, verso mezzanotte, la bella donna trasalì; respingendo dolcemente messer Gilberto, si levò dal letto. Non pareva stanca, ma avviluppata di dolore. Si mosse lentamente e come sorvolando sul suolo verso la porta. "Dove vai?", le gridò con vivo spavento il milite.

The house of sir Gilberto remained locked for the whole day; nobody saw him; nobody knew which mystery he was hiding. The beautiful snow-white arms of the fascinator held him tight in a dream. But, around midnight, the beautiful lady winced; kindly pushing away sir Gilberto, she got up. She didn't look tired, but rather overcome with sorrow. She moved slowly as if she was floating on the ground, towards the door. "Where are you going?", shouted the souldier with serious fright.

Den ganzen Tag über blieb Gilbertos Haus verschlossen, niemand sah ihn, niemand wusste, welches Geheimnis er versteckte. Die schönen, bleichen Arme der bezaubernden Frau hielten ihn in einem Traum fest. Doch gegen Mitternacht fuhr die Schöne zusammen, schob Gilberto sanft von sich und erhob sich aus dem Bett. Sie schien nicht erschöpft, sondern voller Trauer. Sie bewegte sich langsam und wie über den Boden schwebend zur Tür. „Wo gehst du hin?“, rief der Soldat erschrocken.

Ella sospirò, scosse il capo; e le lunghe chiome le ondeggiavano sulle reni. Con un gesto della mano lo salutò; ed il suo gesto non prometteva ritorno. Un singhiozzo parve che le squarciasse il petto.

She sighed, and shook her head; the long hairs were streaming over her loins. She wished him goodby with a wave; and this gesture didn't look as she would have ever come back. A sob seemed to tear up her chest.

Sie seufzte, schüttelte den Kopf, die langen Haare umschwangen ihre Hüften. Sie winkte zum Abschied und ihre Geste versprach keine Rückkehr. Ein Schluchzen schien ihre Brust zu zerreißen.

"Non partite!...", gridò supplicando messer Gilberto.

"Don't leave!...", begged sir Gilberto.

“Geh nicht weg!”, flehte Gilberto.

Ma ella salutò ancora, e aprì la porta senza far rumore. Allora il giovane le si parò dinanzi, per fermarla; ma il suo braccio non ebbe forza. Ella uscì sulla strada, avvolta nel manto saraceno, andando lieve e lenta, simile a un fantasma, a un'ombra, senza voltarsi. Rifaceva la strada percorsa la notte innanzi, ed egli la seguiva, col petto ansante, senza poterla raggiungere, senza poterla fermare, come se una forza misteriosa rendesse pesante la sua persona, difficile il suo passo, inerte il suo braccio. "Oh amore, fermati!", gridavale con voce di pianto.

But she wished him goodby with a wave again, and she opened the door without making any noise. Then the young man stood in front of her, with the intention of stopping her; but his arm did not have any strength. She went out on the street, covered with the saracenic mantle, proceeding silently and slowly, like a ghost or a shadow, never turning back. She was walking the same way as the night before, and he was following her, panting, without being able to reach or to stop her, as if a mysterious force was making his body heavy, his steps difficult, and his arm motionless. "Please stop, my love!", he was shouting at her, with sobs in his voice.

Doch sie winkte erneut und öffnete die Tür, ohne ein Geräusch zu verursachen. Da stellte der junge Mann sich ihr in den Weg, um sie aufzuhalten, aber seine Arme waren kraftlos. Sie trat auf die Straße, in den Sarazenermantel eingehüllt, und ohne sich umzuwenden ging sie leise und langsam davon, wie ein Geist oder ein Schatten. Sie nahm denselben Weg wie in der vergangenen Nacht und er folgte ihr schwer atmend, ohne sie einholen zu können, ohne sie aufhalten zu können, als ob eine mysteriöse Kraft seinen Körper beschweren, seine Schritte schwierig und seinen Arm träge machen würde. „Liebste, bleib stehen!“, rief er mit tränenerstickter Stimme.

Ma ella andava ancora, senza rispondere, come trasportata da un carro invisibile.

But she kept going, without answer, as if she was carried by an invisible cart.

Doch sie lief weiter, ohne zu antworten, wie von einem unsichtbaren Wagen getragen.

Messer Gilberto la vide giungere alla Porta di Mare, vide i soldati aprire la porta, vide il bel corpo discendere verso la riva. "Oh amore, fermati!", gridò, ed uscì anch'egli sulla riva, seguendola, col cuore preso da un terrore nuovo, che gli gelava il sangue.

Sir Gilberto saw her arriving at the sea's gate, the soldiers opening the gate, the lovely body going down to the shore. "Please stop, my love!", he shouted, and he also went to the shore, following her, with his heart grasped by a new fear, which made his blood run cold.

Gilberto sah, wie sie an der Porta die Mare ankam, sah, wie die Soldaten das Tor öffneten, sah, wie der schöne Körper zum Strand hinunterging. „Liebste, bleib stehen!“, rief er und folgte ihr zum Ufer, sein Herz von einer neuen Furcht ergriffen, die ihm das Blut in den Adern gefrieren ließ.

Sul lido ella si voltò un'altra volta, un'ultima volta, e lo salutò. Il suo volto era diffuso di angoscia e il suo gesto aveva il supremo sgomento di una separazione eterna; egli aveva perduto la voce ed il moto; una forza misteriosa lo teneva inchiodato sulla sabbia. Vide il manto saraceno scivolar lentamente sulle membra nude, e avvolgersi ai piedi in mille pieghe, dalle quali ella emergeva come un sottile candido stame, fra candidi petali. La vide entrare nell'acqua, e immergersi lentamente, dolcemente... Ella spariva a poco a poco nel fondo cupo del mare; la bella persona discendeva, e per sempre, nei gorghi misteriosi donde era emersa.

At the foreshore She turned back one more time, one last time, and she wished him goodbye. Her face was filled with anguish, and her gesture held the supreme dismay of an eternal farewell; he had lost his voice and the ability of moving; a mysterious force was tying him to the sand. He saw the saracenic mantle sliding down from her body slowly, and wrapping at her feet with thousands folds, from which she was emerging similar to a thin snow-white stamen surrounded by snow-white petals. He saw her walking inside the water, and diving slowly, gently... She was disappearing in the dark sea little by little; the lovely body was going down, and forever!, disappearing in the mysterious eddies from which she emerged.

Am Strand wandte sie sich noch einmal um, ein allerletztes Mal, und winkte. Ihr Gesicht war angstverzerrt und ihre Geste enthielt die tiefe Bestürzung einer Trennung bis in alle Ewigkeit. Er hatte die Stimme verloren und war unfähig, sich zu rühren, eine mysteriöse Kraft hielt ihn an den Sand gefesselt. Er sah den Sarazenermantel langsam von ihren Schultern gleiten und ihre Füße mit tausend Falten bedecken, aus denen sie wie ein schlankes, schneeweißes Staubblatt zwischen schneeweißen Blütenblättern herausragte. Er sah sie langsam und sanft ins Wasser gleiten… Sie verschwand Stück für Stück zum dunklen Meeresgrund, der schöne Körper versank nach und nach in die mysteriösen Strudel, aus denen er gekommen war.

Non si vedeva che il petto, ma le onde lo lambirono, l'avvolsero, lo celarono; non restava che il capo... Egli vide ancora l'inesprimibile angoscia diffondersi sul pallido volto, fermarsi una lagrima sull'occhio... poi un velo d'acqua lo coperse; per un istante ondeggiavano, serpeggiando sulle acqua, immobili le bionde chiome; indi sparvero anch'esse nella cupa notte del mare. Apparve, rapidamente, la mano, si agitò nel tristissimo eterno saluto, e sparve anch'essa; sparve ogni cosa, ogni vestigio, fuor che il manto raccolto in avvolgimenti, simile a un nido vuoto, a un nido abbandonato...

Nothing but the chest was visible, but the waves lapped against it, wrapped it, concealed it; now only the head was left... He saw once again the unutterable anguish showing up on her visage, and a tear stopping on one of her eyes... then a veil of water covered it; for an instant the blonde hairs were swaying in the water, like snakes; thence they also disappeared in the dark sea's night. A hand came into sight rapidly, waved for the eternal sad farewell, and disappeared again; everything disappeared and nothing was left but the mantle gathered in thousand folds, similar to an empty nest, an abandoned nest...

Nun war nur noch die Brust zu sehen, doch die Wellen berührten sie, umspülten sie, bedeckten sie. Jetzt ragte nur noch der Kopf hervor… Er sah noch einmal, wie sich eine unbeschreibliche Furcht auf ihrem blassen Gesicht ausbreitete und ihr eine Träne ins Auge trat…dann wurde sie von einer Welle bedeckt. Einen Moment lang schaukelten und schlängelten sich die blonden Haare auf dem Wasser, dann verschwanden auch sie in der finsteren Nacht des Meeres. Kurzzeitig tauchte eine Hand auf, winkte zum traurigen ewigen Abschied und verschwand wieder, so wie alles verschwand. Einzig der Mantel blieb als faltiges Häufchen zurück, wie ein leeres Nest, ein verlassenes Nest…

E sulla riva, ogni notte, messer Gilberto va chiamando invano la donna misteriosa che gli aveva mostrato e gli aveva tolto, per sempre, la gioia! Per sempre!

And every night, on the shore, sir Gilberto calls in vain the mysterious lady who first showed him what the joy is, but then took it away from him forever! Forever!

Seither sitzt Gilberto jede Nacht am Strand und ruft vergeblich nach dem mysteriösen Mädchen, das ihm gezeigt hatte, was Freude ist, nur um sie ihm für immer zu nehmen! Für immer!
