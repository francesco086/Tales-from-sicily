L'ondina

The little wave

La notte era tiepida e tranquilla, ma senza luna; nel cupissimo cielo scintillavano le stelle, con un tremolio che pareva si volessero spegnere al minimo soffio. La dolce brezza che saliva dal mare, e faceva dondolare lievemente le cime degli alberi sulla spiaggia, non giungeva alle stelle. Ed esse spandevano intorno una luce tenue, dentro la quale si confondevano i contorni delle cose come se il mistero le avesse generate.

The night was warm and peaceful, with no moon; the stars were glittering in the dark sky, flickering as if the slightest breath would suffice to turn them off. A sweet breeze was coming from the sea and was gently swinging the treetops at the beach, but it was not able to reach the stars. The stars' light was feeble, causing hazy outlines, as if every object was coming from the mystery itself.

Non era ancora notte profonda, ma la città pareva deserta; le scolte forse sonnecchiavano nelle torri; e le mura della Kalsa pareva dormissero anche loro.

It was not deep night yet, but the city seemed desert; perhaps the sentries were sleeping inside the towers; and the walls of the Kalsa appeared as they were sleeping too.

Il porto della Cala era chiuso da pesanti catene di ferro, e le galee riposavano, con un lieve dondolio delle antenne, sul molle letto, al sicuro. Forse in quell'ora il magnifico e potente Ruggero, re di Sicilia, studiava gli astri coi suoi astrologi arabi e compilava con Edrisi la sua geografia; egli forse vegliava, ma i cittadini di Palermo dormivano: gli arabi nella Kalsa dalle quattro porte, gli ebrei nella Juzeta, gli amalfitani presso la porta dei Patitelli, i greci intorno alla loro parrocchia di San Nicolò, sullo sprone che cingeva il porto; i signori normanni e italiani nella via Marmorea, la plebe nell'Albergheria e in Seralcadi, i soldati nelle Moschee, alla Galca. Tutti dormivano. Chi non aveva sonno era messer Gilberto, milite normanno, al quale era stata affidata la custodia della torre di Baich e della Porta di mare.

The Cala's harbour was locked with heavy iron chains, and the galleys were resting in a safe place, while the yards were rocking lightly. Perhaps in that moment the magnificent and powerful Ruggero, king of Sicily, was reading the stars with his arab astrologers and filling in some geographical map with Edrisi; he was possibly awake, but the dwellers of Palermo were asleep: the arabs in the Kals with its four gates, the jews in the Juzeta, the amalfitans at the Patitelli's gate, the greeks in the surroundings of their San Nicolò parish, on the spur that enclosed the harbour; the italian and norman lords in Marmorea Street, the mob at the Albergheria and in Seralcadi, the soldiers at the Galca. Everyone was sleeping. The only one couldn't sleep was sir Gilberto, a norman soldier, who was entrusted with the task of taking care of the Baich tower and of the sea's gate.

Avea posto le guardie dietro la saracinesca; si era assicurato che ogni cosa era in ordine, e se ne era andato a letto. Ma le guardie che aveva a vegliare, dormivano, ed egli che voleva dormire, vegliava suo malgrado. Sentiva caldo. Aveva un bel voltarsi nel letto, i cuscini gli parevano di brace; la volta bassa della stanza l'opprimeva; l'aria chiusa lo soffocava. Allora si alzò e aperse la finestra; si affacciò e guardò il mare ampio, solenne, fresco. Le spume argentine e il mormorio soave dei flutti lo chiamavano: egli lo sentiva; gli pareva che dal fondo di quelle acque una voce lo invitasse: "Vieni, o figlio di Normandia, vieni; qui è la frescura, qui è la dolcezza; vieni, ti comporrò un letto di alghe e ti cullerò nel sonno; vieni!".

He placed the guards behind the portcullis; he made himself sure that everything was in order, and he went to bed. But the guards who had to watch over were asleep, and he, who just wanted to sleep, was awake. He was feeling warm. He was turning around in the bed, and the pillows seemed made of embers to him; the vaulted ceiling was too low and was oppressive; the foul air was stifling him. Then he stood up and opened the window; he leaned out and looked at the broad, solemn, fresh sea. The silvered foams and the sweet murmuring of the waves were calling him: he could hear that; it seemed to him as a voice from the sea bed was inviting him: "Come, oh son of Normandie, come; here is the coolness, here is the sweetness; come, I will compose for you a bed of seaweed and I will lulled you to sleep; come!".

Messer Gilberto guardò per un pezzo quel mare sereno, carezzevole, pieno di misteriosa promessa, e sentì più che mai il peso della volta che l'opprimeva e l'afa insopportabile della stanza: e allora, borbottando, indossò la tunicella e uscì. "Sia che ci voglia, vo' prendere un bagno.".

Sir Gilberto looked for a while at that sea, calm, gentle, full of mysterious promise, and he felt even more the weight of the ceiling oppressing him and the sultriness of his room: and then, while mumbling to himself, he put on his tunic and went out. "I definitely need this, I am going taking a bath".

Diede uno strattone alla guardia che sonnecchiava alla porta. "Che ti venga il malanno, poltrone! Su! Destati!".

He jerked the guard at the gate who was dozing. "Darn you, lazybones! Come on! Wake up!".

Il soldato balzò in piedi, si stropicciò gli occhi, sbadigliò. "Scusate... non vi avevo veduto...". "Tira su la saracinesca, ed apri...". "Aprire? Uscite, messere?". "Fa' quello che ti dico, anima di un saraceno!".

The soldier jumped on his feet, he rubbed his eyes, and yawned. "Excuse me... I did not notice you...". "Lift the shutter, and open...". "Open? Are you going out, sir?". "Do what I am telling you to do, son of a saracen!".

Brontolando, il soldato afferrò la manovella, sollevò la saracinesca, e aprì lo sportello. "Stai attento, che fra un'ora tornerò.". "Buona fortuna, messere" sogghignò il soldato, il quale suppose che il milite messer Gilberto andasse a un ritrovo amoroso.

Grumbling, the soldier grabbed the crank, lifted the shutter, and opened the gate. "Watch out, I will be back in one hour.". "Good luck, sir" sneered the soldier, who was assuming that sir Gilberto was going out for a love affair.

Messer Gilberto discese alla riva, si calò dagli scogli sull'arena molle e fine, si spogliò e si tuffò nel mare con un senso di voluttà grandissima, e con meraviglia del soldato che lo aveva seguito con lo sguardo.

Sir Gilberto went down to the seashore, he shined down the reef to the soft, fine-grained sand, he undressed himself, and he dived into the sea, with his soul filled with pleasure. The soldier, who was following sir Gilberto with his eyes, was surprised.

Cominciò a nuotare silenziosamente, lentamente, ora adagiandosi sopra un piano, ora lasciandosi trasportare dalle onde. Ogni tanto si sommergeva, spariva, per ricomparire alcune bracciate più in là, tutto molle, coi capelli incollati, qualche filo d'erba marina fra le ciocche. Scuoteva allora il capo, e con una vigorosa spinta del piede fendeva le acque. Di quando in quando si fermava, sorreggendosi col remigar lento delle braccia, e guardava innanzi a sè le mura della città, e dietro le mura la corona dei monti, come una gran massa nera, incerta; sul capo l'immenso cielo punteggiato di stelle: un padiglione che non finiva mai; dietro di sè il mare immenso anche esso, che si confondeva col cielo in un abbraccio; e in quel mistero oscuro di cielo e di mare, egli si sentiva piccino, smarrito, soggiogato da un religioso terrore, che lo costringeva a chiudere gli occhi o a rivolgersi altrove.

He started swimming silently, slowly, sometimes laying flat, sometimes letting the waves carrying him. From time to time he was plunging, disappearing, and appearing few strokes ahead, soft, with his hairs stuck on his forehead, and few blades of seaweed in his hairs. He was then shaking his head, and he was slicing through the water with an energetic push of his foot. From time to time he was stopping, supporting himself out of the water with slow movements of his arms, and looking at the walls of the city in front of him, and behind the walls a crown of mountains, that looked like a big, black, unclear mass; the sky above his head, all stippled with stars, seemed huge like a never-ending pavilion; behind his back the sea seemed to have no limits, held in an embrace by the sky; plunged in that dark mystery made of sky and sea, he felt small, lost, subjugated by a sort of religious terror, that was forcing him to close his eyes and look somewhere else.

Ma, a un tratto, trasalì. Mentre così assorto si lasciava trasportare dalle onde, qualche cosa di morbido e fine sfiorò le sue mani.

But all of a sudden, he winced. While he was lost in his thoughts and was letting the waves carrying him, something soft and thin brushed against his hands.

Non era un pesce. Strinse il pugno e lo sollevò: erano capelli; dei lunghi capelli biondi che gli si avvolgevano nella mano e nel braccio. Un brivido gelido di spavento gli corse per la persona, gli arrestò il sangue; il suo pugno si dischiuse per un attimo, ma, subito, le sue mani si unirono, afferrarono la bionda chioma emersa dalle acque, un volto e poi le spalle e il seno di una donna, che pareva morta.

It was no fish. He closed his hand and he lifted it up; long blond hairs were all winded around his hand and arm. A shiver ran down his spine, and froze the blood in his veins; he opened his hands only for a moment, and immediately his hands joined and lifted up from the water the head of blonde hair, a face, and then the shoulder and the breast of a woman, who seemed dead.

Vincendo il senso del terrore e il ribrezzo della morte, messer Gilberto strinse con un braccio i fianchi inerti di quella morta, e con l'altro nuotò alla riva. Quando posò i piedi, sollevò quel corpo molle e abbandonato, lo trasse dall'acqua, lo adagiò sulla sabbia. Guardò. Una commozione profonda gli rimescolò il sangue, e un'onda di pietà gli strappò un gemito di rimpianto. Non aveva mai veduto una donna così meravigliosamente bella. E giaceva come morta, col bel capo biondo reclinato sopra una spalla, tutte le membre perfette, nude, nella incosciente inverecondia della morte. Egli ebbe pietà e amore per quella beltà perduta, e la coperse con la sua tunicella; le si chinò accanto, contemplandola, non sapendo che fare. Certo ella era venuta a chiedergli sepoltura cristiana, per non essere divorata dai pesci. Ma quando era annegata? E chi l'aveva buttata in mare?

Overcoming his sense of terror and disgust of the death, sir Gilberto held the hips of the dead with one arm, and used the second arm for swimming until the shore. When he could walk again, he lifted up that lifeless body, dragged it ashore, and leaned it on the sand. He had a better look at it. A deep emotion made his blood run cold, and a rush of merci made him groan for regret. He had never seen such a beautiful lady before. And she was laying dead, with her beautiful blonde head bowed against her shoulder, with her perfect body naked, with the unconscious impudence of the dead people. He left pity and love for the lost beauty, and he covered her with his tunic; he laid next to her, contemplating her, without knowing what to do. For sure she came to him asking for christian burial and for having her body not eaten by the fishes. But when did she drown? And who threw her in the sea?
